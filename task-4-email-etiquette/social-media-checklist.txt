Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

Do's:

Do maintain a professional tone on platforms like LinkedIn; treat it as an ongoing digital resume.

Do double-check your privacy settings regularly to control who sees your personal posts.

Do pause and think critically before sharing or forwarding unverified information.

Do use your platforms to showcase academic projects, like your recent GitHub repositories or coding badges.

Do engage respectfully with peers and faculty, remembering that digital comments leave a permanent footprint.

Don'ts:

Don't post highly personal or sensitive information (like your exact location, phone number, or travel plans) publicly.

Don't use offensive, discriminatory, or unprofessional language, even in private or temporary stories.

Don't complain about university faculty, specific courses, or classmates online.

Don't click on suspicious links or participate in viral "quizzes" that ask for your personal data.

Don't rely on social media as your sole source of news without verifying the facts through reputable outlets.
