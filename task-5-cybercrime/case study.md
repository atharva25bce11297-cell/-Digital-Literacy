Cybercrime Case Study (UPI Fraud)

**The "Fake Customer Support" UPI Scam**

What is the crime? This is a form of social engineering and financial fraud where scammers manipulate victims into authorizing a Unified Payments Interface (UPI) transaction under the guise of providing customer support.

How it typically happens:

1. The Hook: A student faces an issue with a failed food delivery or refund. They search online for the app's customer care number, unknowingly clicking on a fake number posted by scammers on a public forum or manipulated search result.  
2. The Manipulation: The scammer answers, pretending to be an official executive. They apologize for the inconvenience and promise an immediate refund.  
3. The Trap: The scammer tells the student they need to "verify" their bank account to receive the money. They instruct the student to open their UPI app (like Google Pay or PhonePe) and scan a QR code they send via WhatsApp, or they send a "collect request."  
4. The Execution: The scammer convinces the student to enter their UPI PIN, falsely claiming it is required to *receive* the refund.

**Who is targeted**? 

College students and young adults are frequent targets because they make numerous digital micro-transactions daily and often prioritize speed over security when trying to resolve minor financial hiccups.

**Consequences**: 

Once the UPI PIN is entered, money is instantly deducted from the student's linked bank account rather than credited. Because UPI transactions are real-time and irreversible, recovering the stolen funds is incredibly difficult, leading to significant financial loss and emotional distress for the student.
